# Etch-3 / Chamber C RF drift — 12% yield excursion

- **id**: 2026-07-04-etch3c
- **date**: 2026-07-04
- **bay**: Fab #2 · Etch Bay
- **triggering query**: "Yield dropped 12%"

## Problem

- **Symptom**: -12% yield; 5 of 11 lots out of spec on film thickness.
- **Scope**: LOT-0703, 0704, 0707, 0708, 0711 — 27 wafers at risk, all processed on 2026-07-04 between 10:00–12:00.
- **Why it matters**: ~$2.1M exposure if the affected lots ship; downstream steps (CMP) are blocked pending disposition.
- **Why it wasn't caught automatically**: RF power drifted to 2.31 kW, above the ±0.10 kW process band but under the 2.40 kW hard alarm limit — no alert fired.

## Methodology

1. **Quality Inspection** — queried `quality_inspection.csv` for `mean_thk_nm` below the 46.7 nm spec floor (target 48.2 nm). Found 5 failing lots.
2. **Production History** — queried `production_history.csv` for the 5 failing lots, grouped by `step, equipment, chamber`. CVD and CMP steps showed no common tool; Etch step converged on a single equipment+chamber (Etch-3 / C) across all 5, within a 10:00–12:00 window. Passing lots used other chambers.
3. **Machine Sensors** — queried `machine_sensors.csv` scoped to Etch-3 / C over 10:00–12:00, inspected `rf_power_kw` against the 2.10 kW center and 2.40 kW alarm limit. Found a drift peaking at 2.31 kW.
4. **Correlation** — 1 chamber accounts for 100% of failures; RF drift co-occurs with the thickness loss (r² = 0.91) and stayed under the alarm limit, explaining why the excursion slipped through automated monitoring.

Reference specs used: film thickness target 48.2 nm / floor 46.7 nm; etch RF power center 2.10 kW, warn band ±0.10 kW, hard alarm limit 2.40 kW.

## Output

- **Failing lots**: LOT-0703 (45.1 nm), LOT-0704 (44.8 nm), LOT-0707 (45.3 nm), LOT-0708 (44.6 nm), LOT-0711 (45.5 nm) — all below the 46.7 nm floor.
- **Common factor found**: Etch-3 / Chamber C, 10:00–12:00 window — shared by all 5 failing lots and no passing lot.
- **Sensor evidence**: `rf_power_kw` peaked at 2.31 kW (center 2.10 kW, band ±0.10 kW, alarm 2.40 kW) between 10:00–12:00 on Etch-3 / C.
- **Root cause**: `Etch-3 / Chamber C`
- **Confidence**: 97%

## Insight

- **Recommendation**: **HOLD** — LOT-0703, LOT-0704, LOT-0707, LOT-0708, LOT-0711. Do not ship pending re-measure.
- **Isolation order**: ISO-2026-0704-001 — hold, do not process new lots on Etch-3 / Chamber C; recalibrate RF supply and verify with a test wafer before returning to production.
- **Root-cause class**: In-spec drift below the alarm threshold — a monitoring-band gap, not an equipment failure.
- **Generalizable lesson**: The 2.40 kW alarm limit is too wide relative to the process band (±0.10 kW) to catch drifts that still degrade thickness. Consider tightening the warn/alarm thresholds on RF power for Etch-3, and treat "in spec but off-center" drift as a first-class alert case in future excursions.
